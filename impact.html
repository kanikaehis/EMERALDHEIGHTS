SDG 2 is an incredibly strong and profound imperative along multiple dimensions of society, economy, and environment. Thereby, it deals with several of the following very important things:

1. Lower Hunger and Malnutrition: If the attainment of SDG 2 reduces hunger and malnutrition, then better health will be achieved and that is more so for children and vulnerable women.

2. Healthier Living: Healthy and safe food promotes healthier lifestyles. It helps reduce the prevalence rate of diet-related diseases and thus promotes the physical and mental growth.

3. Economic Development: SDG 2 Encourages sustainable farming and hence develops the economy, creates employment, and increases income for small farmers and in rural areas.


4. Ability to Deal with Crises: Building food systems resilience enhances the ability of such systems to better absorb shocks like climate change, natural disasters, and economic-related problems.

5. Weak Sections' Protections: Emphasis on the weaker sections, that is, women and small farmers reflects balance in society and increases economic strength.

6. Environmental Sustainability: Practices in sustainable farming will conserve nature assets, animals, plants, and ecosystems with the goal of reducing environmental damage to the environment from food consumption.

7. Improved Food Safety: Achieving SDG 2 will give everyone access to safe nutritious food at all times, thus reducing food insecurity globally. 8. Global Cooperation: SDG2 encourages global cooperation as it simultaneously fosters cooperation between partners to develop ideas, resources, and good practices geared toward reducing hunger and promotion of sustainable agriculture. This would, in return, make the world healthier, fairer, and sustainable because of the efficient use of SDG 2 along with its benefits for man now and in the future.
