Challenges

Conflict and Instability

In turn, protracted and chronic conflicts strongly hampers the productive and distribution functions of agriculture, so that increasing hunger and higher levels of food insecurity characterized the regions.

Climate Change

Except other factors, extreme climatic events such as hurricanes, drought, and floods; with changed rains patterns and rising global temperatures, impact agricultural productivity and encourage negative ways of constructing food supply chains.


Economic Factors

Fiscal depression and heavy food prices make healthy foods scarce and unaffordable, particularly to vulnerable individuals.

Food Waste

One-third of all food produced globally is either lost or wasted, a development that makes the problem of food insecurity even worse while depriving other very useful resources at the same time.

Availability of Inputs

Since the inputs such as land and credit and technologies as well as market are not directly available to the smallholder farmers, they cannot produce food in surplus.


Nutritional Challenges

One of the key health risks posing difficulties in ensuring that all have adequate nutrition is malnutrition, characterized by conditions of micronutrient deficiencies and obesity. 7. Global supply chain disruptions: Only lastly, with the COVID-19 epidemic diagnosed, the age-old weaknesses and vulnerabilities in food supply chains have now been officially diagnosed concerning a possible disruptive impact on product availability and consumer access.

Unequal Food Systems

Social and economic inequalities often lead to a situation where the vulnerable groups, particularly women, children, and rural populations, are disproportionately afflicted with hunger and food insecurity. This kind of systemic inequality barrier aggravates the problem by limiting access to enough and nutritious food for these vulnerable groups.

Unfavorable Policies and Investments

It does not invest enough in the agriculture sector, with inappropriate and ineffective policy frameworks therein, which mostly curtails much-needed progress and development concerned with the critical points of ensuring that food security is achieved and sustainable agriculture practices are fostered. These call for the strength of robust, combined global resources with innovative and put-in focus on resilience in building up sustainable food systems for all.
