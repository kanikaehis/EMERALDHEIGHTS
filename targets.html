Eradicate hunger, and ensure that, by 2030, all people - particularly those who are most vulnerable - will have access to sufficient, safe, nutritionally adequate food at all times throughout the year.


SDG 2.1 Indicators

1. Prevalence of Undernourishment This is the proportion of population whose energy intake over a certain period averages less than what is needed to meet minimum requirements.
2. Prevalence of food insecurity It is measured by the food insecurity experience scale, which measures an individual's perception concerning the level of food insecurity in the population.

Eradication of Hunger Commitment

Under subobjective SDG 2.1, it is the aim of the international community to have Zero Hunger. This would ensure that by 2030 nobody has hunger and all people enjoy safe and nutritious food throughout the year, especially the poor and vulnerable.

Among other reasons, under SDG 2.1, conflicts, climatic issues, and economic challenges deny 735 million people their opportunity to proper and nutritious food. Vulnerability policy adopted in partnership with communities under the SDG 2 carries a targeting of vulnerable as the approach to expose the root cause of food insecurity.

Healthy soils are tiny ecosystems with millions of microbes and bacteria; it is, therefore, possible to grow nutrient-dense foods in such soils. Such soils can also hold much atmospheric carbon, make them more resilient during severe drought and other harsh weather events, and reduce soil erosion. The diversity in diets based on biodiverse soils provides the optimal micronutrients, vitamins, and minerals necessary for the nutrition of human beings.
